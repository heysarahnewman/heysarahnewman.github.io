Reading Log

What I've been reading recently.

2026

The Covenant of Water

Abraham Verghese

Loved Cutting for Stone and especially My Own Country — looking forward to this one. Currently in progress!

The Last Samurai

Helen DeWitt

I loved The English Understand Wool beyond words, so was excited for this one - not as into it! Seven Samurai is on my movie watchlist now, though.

2025

Hamnet

Maggie O'Farrell

Yes, I read it in preparation for the movie. I liked it! I admired what O'Farrell did with just church and property records and her imagination. I did find the writing a little overwrought.

The Country Girls

Edna O'Brien

Part of my enduring interest in sad Irish fiction. Written in 1960 but O'Brien understood and even predicted modern girlhood: "Tell a woman that you love her and she'll ask you to write it down so that she can show it to her friends." Girls yearned for the dating app screenshot before it existed!

Glorious Exploits

Ferdia Lennon

I thought this was charming! I should probably go see some Greek tragedy live to really appreciate this.

The March

E.L. Doctorow

Recommended from a podcast on the Civil War I listened to this summer - excellent historical fiction!

Perspectives

Laurent Binet

A murder mystery, involving the Italian Mannerist masters, AND it's an epistolary novel? Nothing better.

Sister Europe

Nell Zink

I love her dispatches from Berlin.

The Time of the Child

Niall Williams

I loved This is Happiness so much that I am practically Evangelical about it. (You should read it, by the way!!) Imagine my delight when I found the sequel on the library "Just In" shelf. So beautifully written, I forgive the treacly sweetness.
